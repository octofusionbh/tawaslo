# Tawaslo vs Hootsuite — Feature Gap Analysis & Roadmap

A category-by-category look at what Hootsuite offers, what Tawaslo already has, and exactly what to build to match or beat them. The encouraging headline: Tawaslo already covers most of the core platform. The real gaps are a focused list of roughly eight items, not a mountain.

---

## The short version

Tawaslo already matches Hootsuite on publishing, scheduling, the visual planner, a unified inbox, AI captions and ideas, hashtags, social listening, analytics, client-ready reports, multi-client workspaces, and a media library. It also beats Hootsuite on two things Hootsuite cannot do: true bilingual Arabic and English with right-to-left, and AI image generation built into the composer.

The genuine gaps are: approval workflows, competitor benchmarking, more publishing platforms, real audience-data best times, bulk scheduling, deeper team permissions, Google Business reviews, and a few security niceties.

---

## Category by category

### Publishing and scheduling

- Hootsuite: schedules to Instagram, Facebook, LinkedIn, X, Pinterest, YouTube, TikTok, Threads. Bulk upload. Best time to post.
- Tawaslo: publishes to Facebook and Instagram live (feed, Reel, Story, carousel). Visual planner with drag and drop. Best-time heatmap per platform. Multi-account posting.
- To build: bring TikTok, LinkedIn, Pinterest, YouTube, and Threads from "coming soon" to live. Add bulk scheduling (upload many posts at once). Swap the heuristic best times for real audience data once Instagram insights are approved.

### Content and AI

- Hootsuite: AI captions (OwlyGPT), hashtag suggestions, content ideas.
- Tawaslo: AI captions in English and Arabic, post ideas, hashtag packs, plus AI image generation in the composer (Hootsuite has no native image generation).
- To build: nothing critical. You are ahead here.

### Analytics and reporting

- Hootsuite: real-time analytics, custom report builder, export to PDF, CSV, XLSX, and PowerPoint, paid-and-organic ROI, and tie-ins to Google Analytics.
- Tawaslo: per-account analytics, adaptive top-posts table, narrative insights, downloadable magazine-style PDF reports.
- To build: add CSV and XLSX export alongside the PDF. Competitor benchmarking is the bigger gap (see below).

### Competitor benchmarking (a real gap)

- Hootsuite: add up to 5 to 20 competitor profiles and compare follower growth, posting frequency, engagement, and content mix automatically.
- Tawaslo: not yet.
- To build: a benchmarking view where an agency adds a client's competitors and sees side-by-side follower growth, posting cadence, and engagement. High value for agencies pitching clients.

### Social listening

- Hootsuite: track mentions, hashtags, keywords, sentiment, trend detection.
- Tawaslo: Streams (live monitoring of mentions, hashtags, keywords) and Listening.
- To build: add sentiment scoring (positive, neutral, negative) on mentions. Otherwise on par.

### Inbox and engagement

- Hootsuite: unified inbox for comments and DMs across networks, auto-assign messages to teammates, plus Google Business Profile reviews.
- Tawaslo: unified inbox for Instagram and Facebook comments and DMs, with threaded replies.
- To build: assign a conversation to a teammate. Add Google Business reviews (great for the restaurants and local brands in the GCC). Saved reply templates.

### Team and collaboration (the biggest agency gap)

- Hootsuite: role-based permissions, departmental structure, and automated approval workflows so a manager or client signs off before a post publishes.
- Tawaslo: a team page and multi-client workspaces, but no formal approval flow.
- To build: approval workflows (draft, submit for approval, approve or request changes, then publish) and granular per-role and per-client permissions. This is the single most important agency feature on the list.

### Security

- Hootsuite: two-factor authentication, single sign-on, granular permissions, instant suspend of scheduled posts.
- Tawaslo: secure auth, plus a new inactivity auto-logout.
- To build: two-factor authentication, and a one-click "pause all scheduled posts" safety switch. Single sign-on later for enterprise.

### Integrations

- Hootsuite: Canva, Google Drive, Dropbox, link shorteners, influencer tools, and many more.
- Tawaslo: Gemini for AI images, Supabase storage, Meta, Tap payments coming.
- To build: a link-in-bio page with click tracking, a link shortener, and a Canva or Google Drive asset import in the composer.

---

## What Tawaslo already does better

- True bilingual Arabic and English with right-to-left throughout. Hootsuite does not.
- AI image generation built into the composer. Hootsuite has none.
- Local and regional payment methods via Tap (Benefit, KNET, and more), built for the GCC.
- Flat, transparent pricing without per-seat fees.
- A cleaner, more modern interface.

---

## Suggested build order

### Now (highest value, mostly independent of Meta approvals)

1. Approval workflows. The defining agency feature Hootsuite has and you do not.
2. Competitor benchmarking. High-impact in client pitches and demos.
3. Bulk scheduling. A clear time-saver for agencies running many clients.

### Next (needs Meta insights approval or platform integrations)

4. Real audience-data best times (auto-upgrades once insights land).
5. More publishing platforms: TikTok, LinkedIn, then Pinterest, YouTube, Threads.
6. Inbox upgrades: assign-to-teammate, saved replies, Google Business reviews.

### Later (polish and enterprise)

7. Analytics export to CSV and XLSX, plus sentiment scoring in listening.
8. Security: two-factor authentication, pause-all-scheduled switch, then single sign-on.
9. Link-in-bio and link tracking, Canva and Google Drive import.

---

## Bottom line

You are not far behind. On the core daily workflow an agency actually uses, publish, plan, engage, analyze, report, in two languages, Tawaslo is already competitive, and ahead on bilingual support and AI images. Shipping approval workflows and competitor benchmarking would close the two gaps that matter most to agencies, and the rest is a steady checklist rather than a rebuild.
